üß† What is Usability Testing?

Usability testing is the process of evaluating your design by testing it with real users. The goal is to uncover issues in:

  • Navigation

  • Content clarity

  • Interaction flow

  • Overall user experience

üèÉ When to Conduct Usability Testing?

  • After wireframing, to confirm the basic structure

  • After high-fidelity design, before development starts

  • During development, to catch new problems

  • After launch, to ensure ongoing usability improvements

üß≥ Types of Usability Tests:

Test Type Purpose Tools
Moderated Testing Conducted with a facilitator (live or remote) Zoom, Skype, Lookback
Unmoderated Testing Participants complete tasks independently Maze, UsabilityHub, Hotjar
A/B Testing Compare two versions of the same page/feature Optimizely, Google Optimize
Remote Testing Test users in their own environment Lookback, UserTesting
In-Person Testing Test users in a controlled, real-world setting UserTesting, Dscout

üß∞ Steps to Conduct Usability Testing:

1. Define Your Goals

What do you want to learn?

  • How easy is it for users to sign up?

  • Are the buttons intuitive to press?

  • Can users find the settings page?

2. Create Tasks for Users

Be specific with what users need to do:

  • ‚ÄúCreate a new task in your to-do list.‚Äù

  • ‚ÄúFind and add a product to your shopping cart.‚Äù

  • ‚ÄúNavigate to the ‚ÄòContact Us‚Äô page.‚Äù

3. Recruit Users

  • Choose users who represent your target audience

  • Aim for 5‚Äì10 users for reliable insights

  • Use platforms like UserTesting, Craigslist, or social media

4. Conduct the Test

  • Observe the user performing tasks

  • Record their screen, actions, and reactions (with consent)

  • Ask open-ended questions (e.g., ‚ÄúWhat do you expect to happen when you click that?‚Äù)

5. Analyze the Results

  • Identify pain points (areas where users struggle)

  • Note success rates (did users complete the task?)

  • Gather qualitative feedback (users‚Äô feelings and opinions)

6. Iterate & Improve

  • Use the insights to tweak your design

  • Prioritize changes based on severity

  • After fixing issues, conduct another round of testing

üé• Example Usability Test Flow:

  1. Test Objective: Assess ease of account creation in a fitness app

  2. User Task: Sign up for a new account

  3. Steps:

    • Open the app

    • Tap "Create Account"

    • Fill in details (name, email, password)

    • Submit and proceed to next screen

  4. Observation: Does the user find the "Create Account" button? Are they confused by any fields?

üìö Tools for Conducting Usability Testing:

  • Lookback: Record user sessions and get live feedback

  • UserTesting: Professional panel of testers with feedback on your product

  • Maze: Create tasks, gather feedback, and track metrics

  • Hotjar: Heatmaps, session recordings, and user feedback polls

  3. Ask open-ended questions after they complete the task (e.g., ‚ÄúDid you find anything confusing?‚Äù).

‚úÖ Best Practices:

  • Keep it small: Test only 1‚Äì2 core features

  • Keep it simple: Provide clear instructions, but don‚Äôt influence users

  • Observe, don‚Äôt interrupt: Let users navigate freely, only step in when necessary

  • Ask for feedback: Get users‚Äô opinions on both the design and the overall experience

‚ùå Common Mistakes:

  • Testing too late in the design process

  • Asking leading questions (e.g., "Wasn't that easy to use?")

  • Having too many participants and not enough variety

  • Ignoring non-verbal cues (frustrated facial expressions)

üìå Pro Tip:
