📘 Day 2: UI/UX Design Process Overview

🎯 Objective:

Understand the end-to-end process of UI/UX design, from initial research to the final product. Learn how structured thinking and iteration lead to great user experiences.

🔄 Why a Design Process Matters

The UI/UX design process ensures that what you build:

  • Solves real user problems
  • Is easy to use and visually pleasing
  • Can be iterated on and improved over time

It's not just about making things look good—it's about designing with purpose.

🛠️ The 6-Step UI/UX Design Process

1. Research

Gather data about users, the market, and the problem.

  • User Interviews, Surveys, Competitor Analysis
  • Example: If you're designing a food delivery app, research what users expect (speed, variety, tracking).
🔧 Tool: Google Forms, Typeform

2. Define (Empathize + Analyze)

Clarify who your users are and what they want.

  • Create Personas: Fictional representations of target users.
  • Build Empathy Maps: Capture what users say, think, feel, and do.

🧠 Example Persona:

Name: Farhan, Age 28
Goals: Order food fast during lunch break
Frustrations: Slow delivery, poor app UX

3. Ideate

Generate solutions. Sketch out ideas. Brainstorm.

  • Crazy 8s method: Fold paper into 8 sections and sketch 8 ideas quickly.
  • Prioritize ideas using the MoSCoW method: Must Have, Should Have, Could Have, Won't Have
💡 Example: Brainstorm multiple homepage layouts for a bookstore app.

4. Wireframe

Create low-fidelity screens to structure content and layout.

  • Focus on structure, not visuals.
  • Use grayscale colors and simple shapes.
🧰 Tool: Figma, Balsamiq, Whimsical
📄 Output: Wireframes of key screens (e.g., Home, Cart, Checkout)

5. Prototype

Build a clickable version of your design.

  • Mid- to high-fidelity designs with interactivity
  • Simulate real user interactions (e.g., tapping a button navigates to another page)
🛠️ Tool: Figma (Prototype Mode), Adobe XD
📱 Output: Working flow through major screens

6. Test & Iterate

Get feedback, find pain points, and refine your design.

  • Conduct usability tests (5 users is often enough to find 80% of issues)
  • Ask: Can the user complete the task without frustration?
🔁 Iterate until usability is smooth and intuitive.

🧭 Flowchart: UI/UX Design Cycle 

Research → Define → Ideate → Wireframe → Prototype → Test → Iterate (loop)

💡 Real-World Example: Netflix

  1. Research: Netflix used viewing data to understand user habits.
  2. Define: Users want quick access to personalized recommendations.
  3. Ideate: Introduced "Continue Watching" and "Top Picks for You."
  4. Wireframe: Designed layout showing rows of thumbnails.
  5. Prototype: Clickable carousel of content.
  6. Test: A/B tested different layouts with real users.

🔄 Mini Challenge / Test Case:

Task: Design Process Case Study

Choose a simple app idea (e.g., habit tracker or grocery list app) and briefly outline each step in the design process.

Example: Grocery List App

  1. Research: Users forget what to buy and want to reuse old lists.
  2. Define: Target users are busy parents who shop weekly.
  3. Ideate: Auto-suggest previous items, voice input.
  4. Wireframe: Sketch home screen with "Add Item" button.
  5. Prototype: Link buttons in Figma.
  6. Test: Share with 3 people and collect feedback.

📝 Summary & Notes:

  • The UI/UX design process is user-centered and iterative.
  • Each phase builds on the previous: research → define → ideate → wireframe → prototype → test.
  • Real products (like Airbnb, Netflix, Uber) go through multiple rounds of this cycle.
  • The key is iteration: don't expect a perfect product in version 1.
