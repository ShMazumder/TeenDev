Usability Testing & Feedback Collection

📘 Day 19: Usability Testing & Feedback Collection – Validate Your Design with Real Users

🎯 Objective:

Learn the importance of usability testing, how to conduct tests, and how to gather and apply user feedback to improve your designs.

🧠 What is Usability Testing?

Usability testing is the process of evaluating your design by testing it with real users. The goal is to uncover issues in:

  • Navigation
  • Content clarity
  • Interaction flow
  • Overall user experience

🏃 When to Conduct Usability Testing?

  • After wireframing, to confirm the basic structure
  • After high-fidelity design, before development starts
  • During development, to catch new problems
  • After launch, to ensure ongoing usability improvements

🧳 Types of Usability Tests:

Test Type Purpose Tools
Moderated TestingConducted with a facilitator (live or remote)Zoom, Skype, Lookback
Unmoderated TestingParticipants complete tasks independentlyMaze, UsabilityHub, Hotjar
A/B TestingCompare two versions of the same page/featureOptimizely, Google Optimize
Remote TestingTest users in their own environmentLookback, UserTesting
In-Person TestingTest users in a controlled, real-world settingUserTesting, Dscout

🧰 Steps to Conduct Usability Testing:

1. Define Your Goals

What do you want to learn?

  • How easy is it for users to sign up?
  • Are the buttons intuitive to press?
  • Can users find the settings page?

2. Create Tasks for Users

Be specific with what users need to do:

  • “Create a new task in your to-do list.”
  • “Find and add a product to your shopping cart.”
  • “Navigate to the ‘Contact Us’ page.”

3. Recruit Users

  • Choose users who represent your target audience
  • Aim for 5–10 users for reliable insights
  • Use platforms like UserTesting, Craigslist, or social media

4. Conduct the Test

  • Observe the user performing tasks
  • Record their screen, actions, and reactions (with consent)
  • Ask open-ended questions (e.g., “What do you expect to happen when you click that?”)

5. Analyze the Results

  • Identify pain points (areas where users struggle)
  • Note success rates (did users complete the task?)
  • Gather qualitative feedback (users’ feelings and opinions)

6. Iterate & Improve

  • Use the insights to tweak your design
  • Prioritize changes based on severity
  • After fixing issues, conduct another round of testing

🎥 Example Usability Test Flow:

  1. Test Objective: Assess ease of account creation in a fitness app
  2. User Task: Sign up for a new account
  3. Steps:
    • Open the app
    • Tap "Create Account"
    • Fill in details (name, email, password)
    • Submit and proceed to next screen
  4. Observation: Does the user find the "Create Account" button? Are they confused by any fields?

📚 Tools for Conducting Usability Testing:

  • Lookback: Record user sessions and get live feedback
  • UserTesting: Professional panel of testers with feedback on your product
  • Maze: Create tasks, gather feedback, and track metrics
  • Hotjar: Heatmaps, session recordings, and user feedback polls

🧪 Hands-On Task:

Conduct a usability test for your To-Do App (from previous days):

  1. Choose 5-7 users to test it (friends, family, or colleagues).
  2. Provide a simple task: "Create a new task in the app."
  3. Ask open-ended questions after they complete the task (e.g., “Did you find anything confusing?”).
  4. Record their reactions and the time taken to complete the task.
  5. Analyze your findings: Did they struggle with any steps?

✅ Best Practices:

  • Keep it small: Test only 1–2 core features
  • Keep it simple: Provide clear instructions, but don’t influence users
  • Observe, don’t interrupt: Let users navigate freely, only step in when necessary
  • Ask for feedback: Get users’ opinions on both the design and the overall experience

❌ Common Mistakes:

  • Testing too late in the design process
  • Asking leading questions (e.g., "Wasn't that easy to use?")
  • Having too many participants and not enough variety
  • Ignoring non-verbal cues (frustrated facial expressions)

📌 Pro Tip:

While testing, look for micro-interactions that may not be obvious (e.g., button hover states, subtle transitions).

📝 Summary:

  • Usability testing helps validate your designs and catch problems early
  • Focus on real users and real tasks
  • Use feedback to iterate and improve
